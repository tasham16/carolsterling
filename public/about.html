About Me

Hi, I'm Carol! I'm an arts educator and teaching artist and I've spent the over 50 years dedicating my life's work as am advocacy platform for the importants of arts education in the lives of people across all ages. In that time, I have had to opportunity to serve in arts education and leadership positions on local, state, regional, national and international levels

As a consultant, I guide teachers, administrators, curriculum coordinators, after school programs, arts in education programs, arts councils, senior centers, libraries, - anyone designing, developing and evaluating arts programs for all ages!

Additionally, I have developed expertise as an educational puppeteer. I work with pre-service and in-service teachers, undergraduate and graduate students in Education, sharing strategies on how to integrate puppetry to enrich the language arts, social studies, health/nutrition/wellness, and science curricula in grades Pre-K-12.

Accomplishments

Over the course of my career,

  • I Conducted workshops throughout the US, and in many other countries, including: Australia, China, Germany, Great Britain, Israel, Japan, and Spain.
  • I was selected as a Fulbright Program Specialst and conducted arts education workshops in Uganda (2012), and India (2015).
  • I received the Distinguished Service Award for Arts Education presented by the John F. Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts in Washington, D.C.,
  • I was invited to The White House where I was recognized for my contribution to education reform efforts.
  • I formerly served on the Jim Henson Foundation Board and I am continuing to serve as a consultant.
  • I served as a Former Board Member of the Puppetry Guild of Greater New York.
